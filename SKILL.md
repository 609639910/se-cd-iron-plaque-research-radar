# Se-Cd-Fe Iron Plaque Research Radar

## Scope

Track research that can support the user's doctoral direction:

> Microbe- and iron plaque-mediated selenium biofortification and cadmium mitigation in rice grown in Se-Cd co-occurring red soils of southern Jiangxi, with mechanisms and synthetic community construction for safe utilization.

Prioritize the main line:

- Se-Cd-Fe coupling in rice, paddy soils, rhizosphere, roots, grain, and red soils.
- Rice root iron plaque: DCB-Fe, DCB-Cd, DCB-Se, DCB-As, Fe/Mn oxides, ROL, Fe(II)/Fe(III), pH/Eh, SEM-EDS, and root-surface barriers.
- Selenium biofortification and cadmium mitigation: selenite, selenate, nano-Se, organic Se, soil or foliar Se, grain Se target ranges, grain Cd safety limits, and yield or quality risks.
- Rhizosphere microbiome and functional strains: Se transformation, Cd immobilization, siderophore, EPS, biofilm, IAA, P solubilization, Fe cycling, mineralization, and stress tolerance.
- SynCom design for rice, Cd remediation, Se enrichment, plant growth promotion, and field-safe utilization.
- Method platforms: DGT, DCB, BCR/sequential extraction, HPLC-ICP-MS Se speciation, 16S/metagenomics, qPCR colonization, pot/hydroponic/field trials, and literature-derived modelling.

Include adjacent topics when they strengthen the main line:

- As risk, water management, alternate wetting and drying, flooding, climate or regional variables, pH/Eh control, iron amendments, nano-Se materials, machine-learning safety windows, Gannan/Jiangxi Se-rich industry, agricultural land safety utilization policies, and soil remediation applications.

Default lower priority unless the user explicitly asks:

- Pure plant molecular biology without soil/rhizosphere/iron-plaque connection.
- Pure food processing or health-product development.
- Pure metagenomic algorithm papers without an experimental path.
- Pure heavy-metal engineering remediation unrelated to rice safe production.
- Non-rice crops, unless they offer a clear transferable mechanism for Se-Cd-Fe, iron plaque, SynCom, or safety-window design.

For detailed keyword sets, scoring rubrics, variable lists, and output templates, read `references/frontier-radar.md` when producing a radar report, literature synthesis, experiment plan, or paper idea list.

## Workflow

1. Clarify or infer the target mode: daily/weekly radar, deep dive, related work, experiment design, variable extraction, thesis ideation, or manuscript planning.
2. Clarify or infer constraints: time window, target problem, sensor/data/method type, rice growth stage, soil type, Se/Cd/As focus, method platform, venue level, and desired depth.
3. Search current sources whenever freshness matters. For latest papers, verify the true latest date and cite links.
4. Expand queries across both domain and method terms. Search the Se-Cd-Fe main line first, then adjacent terms such as As, water management, nano-Se, DGT, BCR, machine learning, and SynCom.
5. Filter candidates by fit to the doctoral line, mechanism depth, data quality, reproducibility, novelty, and ability to become an experiment or manuscript.
6. Score candidates using relevance, iron-plaque/mechanism fit, method strength, field or pot validity, microbe/SynCom usefulness, safety-window value, reproducibility, and publishable potential.
7. Synthesize compactly first: frontier movement, key papers, and why they matter.
8. Convert findings into action: hypotheses, treatment settings, indicators, sampling design, measurements, baselines, risks, target article type, and reading queue.
9. Distinguish verified source facts from inference. When a paper is only adjacent, explicitly state the transfer path to the user's rice Se-Cd-Fe system.

## Source Priorities

Use primary or near-primary sources whenever possible:

- Papers: PubMed, publisher pages, DOI pages, Web of Science/Google Scholar discoverable pages, ScienceDirect, ACS, Springer, Nature, Wiley, Taylor & Francis, MDPI, Frontiers, Elsevier journal pages, OpenReview, arXiv, bioRxiv, and author project pages.
- Methods and data: NCBI, SRA, MG-RAST, GitHub, protocols, dataset homepages, DGT/DCB/BCR method papers, official standard or policy pages.
- Benchmarks and models: Papers with Code, official GitHub repositories, model pages, and supplementary datasets before third-party summaries.
- Policy and application: Chinese national standards, Jiangxi/Gannan policy pages, agricultural land safe utilization standards, food safety limits, and official laboratory/project pages.

When using web results, cite links in the final answer. For "latest", "recent", "today", "weekly", or time-sensitive claims, verify dates before ranking.

## Report Modes

Use the mode that best matches the request:

- Daily radar: 5-8 items from the last 1-3 days, concise trend notes and immediate relevance.
- Weekly radar: 10-15 items from the last 7-14 days, ranked table, scores, top picks, and research ideas.
- Deep dive: 1-5 papers/projects, with method logic, experimental design, indicators, limitations, reproducibility, and adaptation to the user's topic.
- Experiment design: convert papers into treatments, controls, sampling stages, DCB/DGT/BCR/Se speciation/microbiome indicators, data tables, risk controls, and fallback designs.
- Related work: group papers by mechanism chain: Se-Cd interaction, Fe/iron plaque, rhizosphere microbes, SynCom, water/pH/Eh control, safety windows, and field utilization.
- Thesis/paper ideation: produce 3-5 publishable directions with hypothesis, gap, method sketch, datasets/experiments, metrics, baselines, risks, and target venues.
- Variable extraction framework: define literature extraction fields for Se dose/form, soil properties, pH/Eh, Fe/iron plaque, grain Se/Cd/As, yield, climate, water management, and model-ready outcomes.

## Output Rules

Prefer Chinese when the user writes Chinese. Keep English paper titles unchanged.

For radar reports, include:

- Executive summary: 3-5 bullets on frontier movement.
- Ranked table: title/project, source/date, task, system, contribution, code/data/material status, score, and why it matters.
- Top picks: 3 deeper analyses with method, experiments, limitations, and extension ideas.
- Main-line vs adjacent label: mark each item as main-line, adjacent, or low-priority.
- Transfer notes for adjacent work: transferable component, required changes, likely rice/red-soil datasets or experiments, and risk.
- Research ideas: 3 concrete ideas with hypothesis, method sketch, datasets or experiments, indicators, baselines, risk, and target article type.
- Reading queue: essential papers/projects to read next.

For experiment plans, include:

- Research question and hypothesis.
- Treatment/control design.
- Sampling stage and sample type.
- Mandatory indicators: grain/root/shoot Se and Cd, DCB-Fe/Cd/Se, soil available Se/Cd, pH, Eh, Fe(II)/Fe(III), and yield or biomass where applicable.
- Strongly recommended indicators: DGT, BCR or Se fractionation, HPLC-ICP-MS Se speciation, ROL, SEM-EDS, Feo/Fed, EPS, siderophore, microbial community, functional strain colonization, and As when risk is plausible.
- Risk controls and fallback path: what remains publishable if SynCom, omics, field trial, or advanced instruments fail.

Avoid overclaiming. State when a conclusion is an inference from sources rather than a demonstrated result. If search access is unavailable, state that the answer is based on local/user-provided material and ask for links or permission only when needed.
